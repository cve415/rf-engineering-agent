# RISC-V Academic Ecosystem & Training Latice
**Version:** 1.0 (Public)
**Curated by:** CVE Sourcing in collaboration with RISC-V International Academia & Training

## 1. OVERVIEW
This document maps the leading U.S. academic institutions integrating RISC-V into core engineering curricula and advanced research. These institutions are the primary feeders for the next generation of silicon architects and RTL engineers.

## 2. TOP-TIER RESEARCH & CURRICULUM LEADERS
- **UC Berkeley:** The birthplace of RISC-V; central hub for open-hardware innovation.
- **MIT:** Focus on secure hardware research and advanced computation structures.
- **Stanford:** Specialized in hardware/software interfaces and digital system design.
- **Carnegie Mellon (CMU):** High-assurance and verified system design using RISC-V.
- **UIUC:** Computer Organization and Design; heavy focus on processor labs.
- **University of Michigan:** Advanced Out-of-Order (OoO) CPU design (EECS 470).

## 3. APPLIED ARCHITECTURE & FPGA SPECIALISTS
- **UNLV:** Central driver of the RVfpga initiative.
- **Arizona State (ASU):** FPGA-based system projects and architecture labs.
- **UT Arlington:** Hands-on custom RISC-V processor construction (CSE 4372).
- **Portland State:** Official RISC-V Academia Partner.

## 4. SPECIALIZED INNOVATION HUBS
- **Rice University:** RVR Lab (Microcontrollers to Vector Cores).
- **Penn State:** Space-grade and radiation-hardened RISC-V architectures.
- **Georgia Tech:** Many-core systems and AI accelerator research.
- **Cornell:** Cross-layer hardware/software instruction (CS 3410).

## 5. PROFESSIONAL PIVOT RESOURCES
For experienced engineers transitioning to Open Architecture, we recognize the following certification paths:
- **Linux Foundation:** LFD110 (Intro) and LFD114 (Porting Software).
- **edX/Online:** Build a CPU Core and Industrial RISC-V SoC Architecture.
